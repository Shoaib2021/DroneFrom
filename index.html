Revolutionizing drone swarm technology through innovative, cost-effective solutions for autonomous flight coordination and formation control.

Project Abstract

The AeroForm research project focuses on developing a low-cost solution for UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) flight formation systems. Traditional drone swarm technologies often rely on expensive hardware and complex systems, limiting their accessibility and practical applications. Our research aims to democratize this technology by creating an affordable, scalable platform for coordinated drone flight.

By leveraging off-the-shelf components and open-source software, we've developed a formation control system that maintains precise positioning using a combination of UWB (Ultra-Wideband) for relative localization and RTK-GPS for absolute positioning. Our approach reduces costs by over 60% compared to commercial solutions while maintaining formation accuracy within 30cm.

The system implements a leader-follower architecture with distributed decision-making capabilities. Each drone in the formation communicates with adjacent units using a mesh network topology, ensuring robust communication even in challenging environments. Our adaptive control algorithms enable the swarm to maintain formation during complex maneuvers and respond dynamically to environmental obstacles.

Potential applications include aerial light shows, agricultural monitoring, search and rescue operations, and infrastructure inspection. This research represents a significant step toward making swarm robotics technology accessible to researchers, educators, and commercial developers.

Research Methodology

System Architecture

We developed a hybrid architecture combining centralized planning with decentralized execution. The ground station handles mission planning while drones autonomously execute formation keeping using onboard sensors.

Localization System

Implemented a multi-sensor fusion approach combining UWB for relative positioning (accuracy ±15cm) and RTK-GPS for absolute positioning (accuracy ±2cm) with IMU data for orientation estimation.

Communication Protocol

Developed a custom TDMA-based protocol on 900MHz band enabling low-latency communication between drones with <100ms delay, supporting up to 8 drones in formation.

Control Algorithms

Designed and implemented PID controllers with adaptive gains for formation keeping. Incorporated potential field methods for collision avoidance and formation reconfiguration.

Hardware Implementation

Flight Controller

Custom-designed controller based on Pixhawk 4 running PX4 firmware with extended capabilities for formation flight and swarm communication.

Communication Module

Dual-band communication system with 900MHz long-range telemetry for ground link and 2.4GHz WiFi mesh for inter-drone communication.

Positioning System

UWB Decawave DWM1000 modules for relative positioning and Here+ RTK GPS system for centimeter-level absolute positioning.

Software Stack

PX4 Firmware

Customized for formation flight with extended MAVLink messages

ROS 2

Robot Operating System for high-level control and simulation

Gazebo

Simulation environment for testing formation algorithms

UWB Stack

Custom firmware for Decawave modules with TDoA support

MAVSDK

For ground station control and mission planning
